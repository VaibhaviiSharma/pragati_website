About Jaipur

Jaipur, often referred to as the Pink City, is renowned for its rich history, majestic architecture, and vibrant culture. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur is a city that beautifully blends the past with the present.

As you wander through its busy streets, you'll be mesmerise by the sight of grand palaces, ancient forts, and lively markets that offer a unique shopping experience. From traditional handicrafts to modern-day marvels, Jaipur has something to offer every traveler.

Top Places to Visit in Jaipur

Hawa Mahal

Known as the Palace of Winds, Hawa Mahal is an architectural masterpiece with its incredible latticework and honeycomb structure. Built in 1799, it was designed to allow the royal women to observe street life and festivals from behind the purdah.

City Palace

Located in the heart of Jaipur, the City Palace is a stunning blend of Mughal and Rajput architecture. The palace complex houses museums, courtyards, and gardens, giving visitors a glimpse into the royal lifestyle of the past.

Amber Fort

Perched on a hilltop, Amber Fort is one of the most iconic landmarks of Jaipur. The fort's majestic structure, beautiful mirror work, and the serene Maota Lake make it a must-visit for anyone exploring the city.

Our Experience in Jaipur

Jaipur was an unforgettable journey that took us through the heart of Rajasthan's royal heritage. From the moment we arrived, we were immersed in the city's vibrant energy and rich cultural.

Walking through the streets of Jaipur, we saw the wonderful sight of pink-hued buildings, busy bazaars, and the sound of traditional folk music that seemed to echo from every corner. Our exploration of the Hawa Mahal was beautiful, as we marveled at its great design and the historical significance it holds.

The visit to the City Palace was like stepping back in time. The greatness of the palace, combined with the beautiful artwork and the stories of the Maharajas, made us feel like we were living in a different era. We even had the chance to enjoy a traditional Rajasthani meal within the palace complex, which was a delightful experience in itself.

Our journey continued to Amber Fort, where we witnessed the beauty of Rajasthan's landscape from the fort's towering walls. The evening light and sound show at the fort was a perfect end to our day, as it brought to life the history and legends associated with the fort.

Throughout our trip, the behaviour of the people of Jaipur made us feel welcomed and at home. Whether it was roaming in the markets or enjoying a cup of masala chai with locals, every moment added to the richness of our experience.
